Api sources
coingecko:
1. https://pro-api.coingecko.com/api/v3/global

Response fields we store:
- total_market_cap_usd
- total_volume_usd
- market_cap_percentage.btc (btc_dominance_pct)
- market_cap_percentage.eth (eth_dominance_pct)
- market_cap_percentage.usdt (usdt_dominance_pct) 
- market_cap_percentage.sol (sol_dominance_pct)
- market_cap_change_percentage_24h_usd
Cadence: Every 30 minutes 


2. https://pro-api.coingecko.com/api/v3/simple/price?ids=bitcoin,ethereum,solana,binancecoin,arbitrum,optimism,matic-network,avalanche-2,aptos,starknet,hyperliquid,the-open-network,ripple,cardano,chainlink,mantle&vs_currencies=usd&include_market_cap=true&include_24hr_vol=true&include_24hr_change=true&include_last_updated_at=true

Response fields we store per asset:
- price_usd
- market_cap
- volume_24h
- change_24h_pct
- price_updated_at



3. https://pro-api.coingecko.com/api/v3/coins/bitcoin/market_chart?vs_currency=usd&days=7

https://pro-api.coingecko.com/api/v3/coins/ethereum/market_chart?vs_currency=usd&days=7

What we derive and store:
- Extract the 7 daily closes from the hourly series (one per calendar day at 00:00 UTC)
- Store chart as a JSON array: [{date, close}, ...]
- Derive: week_high, week_low, week_open
- Derive: current_position_in_week_range = (current_price - week_low) / (week_high - week_low) → 0.0 to 1.0 (0 = at week low, 1 = at week high)
- Derive: week_trend_direction = "up" / "flat" / "down" based on first vs. last day close
Cadence: Twice per day at the scheduled pipeline run.Schedule the daily historical fetch for 00:15 UTC and at every ingestion run to get half-day data.


coinbase:
https://api.coinbase.com/v2/prices/{currency_pair}/spot

Our usage: Cross-validation of CoinGecko prices for BTC, ETH, SOL, and other supported pairs. Coinbase's spot price reflects their exchange specifically — it is not volume-weighted across all markets, but it represents a highly liquid US-regulated exchange price.
Supported pairs (relevant to us):
BTC-USD, ETH-USD, SOL-USD, BNB-USD, XRP-USD, ADA-USD, AVAX-USD, 
MATIC-USD, OP-USD, LINK-USD, DOT-USD


binance:
1. https://api.binance.com/api/v3/avgPrice

Our usage: A third price source for the averaging mechanism. Binance's 5-min VWAP is arguably the most accurate available price for assets with high Binance volume (BTC, ETH, SOL, BNB, and most altcoins).
Pairs we'll call:
BTCUSDT, ETHUSDT, SOLUSDT, BNBUSDT, XRPUSDT, ADAUSDT, AVAXUSDT,
MATICUSDT, ARBUSDT, OPUSDT, APTUSDT, STRKUSDT, TONUSDT, LINKUSDT,
MNTUSDT, SCRUSDT, HYPEUSDT

2. https://api.binance.com/api/v3/ticker/24hr

Our usage: The 24h ticker is more information-rich than avgPrice. The fields we need:
- priceChangePercent → change_24h_pct
- 24h_high, 24h_low 
- volume (base asset volume) + quoteVolume 
- weightedAvgPrice
Multi-symbol call (single request for all assets):
GET /api/v3/ticker/24hr?symbols=["BTCUSDT","ETHUSDT","SOLUSDT","BNBUSDT",...]

3. https://api.binance.com/api/v3/klines?symbol=BTCUSDT&interval=1d&limit=14

coinalyze:
1. https://api.coinalyze.net/v1/open-interest?api_key=888d13e2-a7c9-4837-83db-1c6db919ce14&symbols=BTCUSDT_PERP.A&convert_to_usd=true

What we store: Aggregate OI across major exchanges for BTC and ETH perpetuals. Sum all exchange OI values for the same base asset → total_oi_usd.

2. https://api.coinalyze.net/v1/funding-rate

What we store: Current funding rate for BTC and ETH perps on Binance (most liquid), Bybit, and OKX. The simple average of these three is the market consensus funding rate.
Interpretation for the regime deriver:
Funding > +0.05% (per 8h) → extremely crowded longs, high liquidation cascade risk
Funding +0.01% to +0.05% → longs slightly dominant, normal bull market
Funding -0.01% to +0.01% → neutral positioning
Funding < -0.01% → shorts crowded, potential short squeeze setup
Funding < -0.03% → extreme short crowding, very unusual

3. https://api.coinalyze.net/v1/open-interest-history?symbols=BTCUSDT_PERP.A&interval=daily&from={7_days_ago_unix}&to={now_unix}&convert_to_usd=true


Our usage: Daily OI history for BTC over 7 days.
This gives us the 7-day OI trend: is leverage building or collapsing over the week? This is a critical contextual signal.. a 20% OI expansion over 7 days heading into a news event means the market is very leveraged and susceptible to a flush.

4. https://api.coinalyze.net/v1/funding-rate-history

Funding Rate History
Same pattern as OI history. 7-day daily funding rate history for BTC/ETH perps. We derive: average funding rate over 7 days, direction trend (increasing/decreasing/stable).

5. https://api.coinalyze.net/v1/liquidation-history

Liquidation Data
Parameters: symbols, interval, from, to
Schema:
{
  "symbol": "BTCUSDT_PERP.A",
  "history": [
    {"t": 1700000000, "l": 45000000.0, "s": 2000000.0}
  ]
}
Where l = longs liquidated (USD), s = shorts liquidated (USD) in that interval.

What we store: 
- 24h long liquidation total
- 24h short liquidation total
- 24h net liquidation direction (longs_dominated / shorts_dominated / balanced)

6. https://api.coinalyze.net/v1/long-short-ratio-history 

Long/Short Positioning
Schema per interval: {t, r (ratio), l (longs %), s (shorts %)}

What we store: 
- Current long percentage
- current short percentage
- 7-day trend of long/short ratio. 
When longs > 70%, market is crowded long. When shorts > 55%, the squeeze setup is live.

7. https://api.coinalyze.net/v1/ohlcv-history

Perp Price + Volume
This is the OHLCV for perpetual futures specifically (not spot). The fields include v (volume), bv (buy volume), Sell volume = v-bv, and btx (buy trades). The buy/sell volume ratio tells you if there's a systematic market-buy or market-sell flow.. this is the "tape reading" signal. A rising price with sell volume dominant is a trap.


defillama:
1. https://api.llama.fi/v2/chains — TVL by Chain
What it gives us: Current TVL for all chains with gecko_id, tvl, tokenSymbol, name.
Our call: Single call, no parameters.
What we extract (filtered to our tracked chains):
Ethereum TVL
Arbitrum TVL
Base TVL
Optimism TVL
Solana TVL
BNB Chain TVL
Avalanche TVL
Polygon TVL
Starknet TVL
What we store: A JSON dict of {chain_name: tvl_usd} in the market_snapshots extra_json, plus aggregate total DeFi TVL.

2. https://api.llama.fi/v2/historicalChainTvl/{chain} — Historical Chain TVL
What it gives us: Array of {date, tvl} for a specific chain. Goes back to the chain's genesis.
Our usage: For each tracked chain, call this endpoint and extract the last 7 daily data points. Derive: TVL 24h change %, TVL 7d change %, TVL trend direction.

The right API call pattern: Rather than calling this for all 9 chains each cycle, call it once daily (not every 30 minutes — TVL doesn't change meaningfully every 30 minutes). Schedule as a bi-daily companion to the OHLCV fetch.

3. https://stablecoins.llama.fi/stablecoins

Stablecoin Market Data
What it gives us: All stablecoins with name, symbol, circulating (by peg type), price, chains, chainCirculating.
Our usage: Extract USDT, USDC, DAI, USDE (Ethena), FRAX circulating supply. Sum total stablecoin circulating supply. Calculate stablecoin market dominance (stablecoin_total / total_crypto_mcap from CoinGecko global). Store 7-day trend.

4. https://stablecoins.llama.fi/stablecoinprices

Historical Stablecoin Prices
What it gives us: Historical price data for all stablecoins. Critical for depeg detection.
Our usage: Fetch daily. If USDT, USDC, or DAI price deviates > 0.3% from $1.00 in the latest data, set a stablecoin_depeg_alert: true flag in the market snapshot. This flag gets propagated directly into the AI prompt as a high-severity marker regardless of whether any news signal mentioned it.

5. https://api.llama.fi/overview/dexs?excludeTotalDataChart=true&excludeTotalDataChartBreakdown=true

DEX Volume Overview
What it gives us: Total DEX volume 24h, 7d change, protocol breakdown with individual volumes.
Our usage: Total DEX 24h volume, DEX volume 7d trend (rising/falling/flat), and the on-chain/off-chain activity ratio (compare DEX volume to total crypto 24h trading volume from CoinGecko).

6. https://api.llama.fi/overview/fees?excludeTotalDataChart=true&excludeTotalDataChartBreakdown=true

Protocol Fees
What it gives us: Total fees generated by all DeFi protocols in the last 24h. Protocol-level breakdown.
Our usage: Total DeFi fees 24h is a "DeFi productivity" metric — how much real value is being generated on-chain. Top 5 fee-generating protocols tell you what people are actually using (not just holding TVL in).

IMF: 
1. https://api.imf.org/external/sdmx/2.1/data/IMF.STA,CPI/USA.CPI._T.SRP_YOY_PCH_PA_PT.M?startPeriod=2023-01&endPeriod=2026-03&lastNObservations=13&detail=dataonly

Consumer Price Index
Key series we need:
- US CPI, All Items (monthly, index)

What we store: Latest monthly CPI value, YoY change %, 3-month trend direction (accelerating/stable/decelerating).

2. 
https://api.imf.org/external/sdmx/2.1/data/IMF.RES,WEO/USA+G001.LUR+NGDP_RPCH+PCPIEPCH.A?startperiod=2021&endPeriod=2027&lastNObservations=7&detail=dataonly

WEO — World Economic Outlook
What it is: Published twice yearly (April and October). Contains GDP growth forecasts, inflation projections, unemployment, and debt ratios for all IMF member countries and aggregates.

Key series:
US.NGDP_RPCH — US Real GDP Growth (annual % change)

US.PCPIEPCH — US End-period CPI inflation

G001.NGDP_RPCH — World Real GDP Growth (the most important global context)

US.LUR — US Unemployment Rate

What we store: The most recent WEO publication's projections for current and next year. This does not change between publications, so we store it once and only update when a new WEO is published (April/October).

Note: Accurate data is passed via XML, we would be building a different parsing structure for tge IMF data alone.
